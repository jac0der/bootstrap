Discover Jamaica

FAQs

This picturesque country is situated in the West Indies, an archipelago located between the southeastern United States and northern South America. Jamaica lies in the Northern and Western Hemispheres, around 17 degrees North latitude and between 77 and 79 degrees West longitude. It is surrounded by the Caribbean Sea. Jamaica's nearest neighbors are Cuba, 90 miles (140 km) to the north, and Haiti, 118 miles (190 km) to the east.

The official currency of Jamaica is the Jamaican dollar (JMD). On the island, money can be exchanged at commercial banks, Cambios, and hotels/resorts. Currency exchange is also available at the airports in Montego Bay and Kingston. Cambios, banks, and larger hotels/resorts accept US dollars, Euros, British pounds, and Canadian dollars. However, relying on ATMs can be somewhat risky in Jamaica. Most banks in cities like Kingston and Montego Bay have 24-hour ATMs located in secure booths.

If you are planning to travel to Jamaica, here are some of the airlines that will take you to Jamaica from these countries.

  • London: TUI, British Airways, American Airlines, Delta, Virgin Atlantic, Air Canada and West Jet offer flights from London to Jamaica.
  • USA: Southwest Airlines, JetBlue, Delta, and Spirit Airlines offer flights from the USA to Jamaica.
  • Dubai: JetBlue and British Airways offer flights from Dubai to Jamaica.
  • Singapore: British Airways and United Airlines offer flights from Singapore to Jamaica.
